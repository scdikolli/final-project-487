About the Tour

From May 8th – October 6th, Taylor Swift performed the Reputation Stadium Tour across America. Swift traveled to 22 states, highlighting major cities including Glendale, Arizona, East Rutherford, New Jersey, Atlanta, Georgia, and Arlington, Texas. On November 30th, the tour was announced as the highest-grossing in American history, grossing over $250 million from more than 2 million tickets sold to fans across the country.

The Reputation Stadium Tour consisted of 38 shows across 26 different cities in the country. Most notable about these tour stops are the chosen venues. The smallest venue for the Reputation Stadium Tour was Hard Rock Stadium in Miami Gardens, Florida with a capacity of just over 47,000 people. The largest venue? Gillette Stadium, which held three different shows for the star, in Foxborough, Massachusetts holding a maximum capacity of 174,000.
